## About Auto Clicker

Auto Clicker is a Safari extension for macOS that automates repetitive clicking tasks on web pages. Set custom click intervals, target specific page elements, and automate workflows that would otherwise require tedious manual clicking — saving time on form filling, testing, and repetitive browser tasks.

Many web-based workflows involve repetitive clicks: filling out forms, clicking through multi-step processes, testing web applications, or interacting with page elements at regular intervals. Auto Clicker eliminates this manual effort by letting you configure automated click patterns that run on your behalf.

Configurable click speed and patterns give you precise control over the automation. Keyboard shortcuts let you start and stop clicking instantly, and the ability to target specific elements means you can automate complex interactions, not just blind clicking at fixed coordinates.

## Why choose Auto Clicker?

Auto Clicker combines simplicity with precision. The custom interval settings and element targeting give you fine-grained control over exactly what gets clicked and when, while the keyboard shortcuts keep operation smooth and quick.

As a Safari extension, it requires no additional software installation or complex setup. It is the most accessible way to automate repetitive clicking tasks in your browser on macOS.

## How to Use Auto Clicker

Getting started takes less than a minute:

1. **Install and enable the extension.** After installing from the App Store, open Safari → Settings → Extensions and turn on Auto Clicker.
2. **Set your click interval.** Choose how often clicks fire — from a rapid cadence to once every few seconds.
3. **Choose a target (optional).** Point Auto Clicker at a specific page element so it clicks the right button or link instead of a fixed screen position.
4. **Start and stop with a keyboard shortcut.** Trigger clicking with a shortcut and stop it instantly with the same key — no need to reach for the mouse.

Because the automation runs inside Safari, there is nothing extra to install and no separate app running in the background.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Does Auto Clicker work on any website?**
Yes. It runs as a Safari extension, so it works across the web pages you open in Safari on macOS.

**Can I click a specific button instead of a fixed spot on screen?**
Yes. Auto Clicker can target specific page elements, so it clicks the intended control even if the layout shifts — not just blind coordinates.

**How do I stop the clicking?**
Use the keyboard shortcut. The same shortcut that starts the automation stops it immediately.

**Do I need any other software?**
No. Auto Clicker is a self-contained Safari extension. Once it is enabled in Safari's settings, there is nothing else to install or configure.
