## About Retrofoot

Retrofoot is a top-down arcade football game built as a love letter to the late-80s and early-90s coin-op era. Every sprite, every kit, every stadium is hand-painted in a tight retro palette — no 3D models, no motion blur, no bloat. The fixed top-down camera, twitchy joystick, and three-button action layout mean every match is instantly readable and over in a sitting. Pick a country, pick a formation, and play.

The game ships with three full modes. **Tournament** is a single-elimination 16-team knockout — extra time and penalty shootouts settle every drawn knockout match, and lifting the trophy puts your name on the wall. **League** is a 12-club European campaign across eleven matchdays, with a full table tracking points, goal difference, and head-to-head tie-breakers; you can resume your season any time. **Quick Match** lets you pick two sides and play on the spot, with half lengths configurable from three minutes to fifteen. **Local Head-to-Head** pairs two devices over Wi-Fi or Bluetooth so you can settle a grudge without accounts, online matchmaking, or waiting in a lobby.

Retrofoot follows the classic free-to-try, one-time-unlock model. The free download includes Quick Match. A single in-app purchase unlocks Tournament, League, and Local Head-to-Head — forever, on every device tied to your Apple ID. No consumables, no energy bars, no rewarded video, no subscription.

## Who is Retrofoot for?

- Football fans who grew up with 16-bit pixel football and want the same feel on a modern phone
- Anyone who prefers tight, twitchy arcade controls over modern simulation menus and microtransactions
- Commuters and casual players looking for a clean three-minute match on the train
- Friends who want a real local multiplayer match without joining a service or signing in
- Players who use MFi, PlayStation, or Xbox controllers with their iPhone or iPad

## Why choose Retrofoot?

Retrofoot is built around the belief that arcade football peaked when matches were short, controls were sharp, and one save game lasted a season. The fixed top-down camera makes the entire half of play visible at all times, so positioning, runs, and through-balls are about reading the pitch — not chasing a tracking camera. Context-aware action buttons mean tackles, passes, and shots all live exactly where your thumb expects them, and full controller support gives you a real button layout when you want it.

Behind the chunky pixel sprites, the rules are complete: throw-ins, goal kicks, corners, offsides, fouls, free kicks, penalties, the keeper back-pass rule, and the keeper six-second rule are all in. Extra time and penalty shootouts decide knockout draws. The keeper dives, parries, and rushes off his line. Teammates make runs and defenders cover. Three difficulty tiers let you scale from a relaxed warm-up to a genuine fight, and slow-motion goal replays from the perfect angle let you savour the best moments before the next kickoff.

For local multiplayer, Retrofoot uses Apple's Multipeer Connectivity to pair two devices directly over Wi-Fi or Bluetooth — no servers, no accounts, no internet required. Bring your own opponent, take the home or away kit, and play. It is football the way it used to feel: pick a team, pick a fight, kick off.
