## Making the Web Accessible for Everyone

The web was designed to be universal, but in practice, many websites fail to accommodate users with visual impairments, reading difficulties, or cognitive differences. Small text, poor contrast, cluttered layouts, and fonts that are difficult to read can turn web browsing from a useful activity into an exhausting one.

Safari accessibility extensions bridge this gap by letting you modify how web pages appear on your screen. Whether you have dyslexia, presbyopia (age-related vision changes), or simply find most websites hard to read comfortably, these extensions put you back in control of your reading experience.

## Dyslexia: Making Web Text Readable

An estimated 15-20% of the population has some degree of dyslexia, a learning difference that affects how the brain processes written language. Standard web fonts can make reading significantly harder for people with dyslexia, as letters appear to move, swap, or blur together.

[Dyslexia](/apps/dyslexia/) is a Safari extension designed specifically to address these challenges. It modifies web page text to be more readable for people with dyslexia by:

- **Applying dyslexia-friendly fonts** that weight the bottom of letters more heavily, reducing the tendency for letters to visually rotate or mirror.
- **Adjusting letter spacing** to reduce crowding, which is a major contributor to reading difficulty in dyslexia.
- **Modifying line height** to give each line of text more visual breathing room.
- **Offering customizable text size** so you can find the size that works best for you.

### Setting Up Dyslexia for Safari

1. Install the Dyslexia extension from the App Store.
2. Enable it in Safari > Settings > Extensions (Mac) or Settings > Apps > Safari > Extensions (iPhone).
3. Visit any web page and activate the extension.
4. Customize the font, spacing, and size settings to match your preferences.
5. Your settings will be remembered and applied automatically across all websites.

## Presbyopia: Browsing with Low Vision

Presbyopia is the gradual loss of near focusing ability that typically begins around age 40 and affects virtually everyone as they age. Reading small text on web pages becomes increasingly difficult, and constantly zooming in and out is not a practical long-term solution.

[Presbyopia](/apps/presbyopia/) is a Safari extension that addresses age-related vision changes by:

- **Enlarging text** across all web pages to a comfortable reading size without breaking page layouts.
- **Improving contrast** between text and background colors, making content easier to distinguish.
- **Adjusting color schemes** to reduce visual strain associated with certain color combinations.
- **Providing quick magnification** controls right in the toolbar.

This extension is particularly useful because it applies changes intelligently. Rather than simply zooming the entire page (which requires horizontal scrolling), Presbyopia increases text size while maintaining readable line widths and page structure.

## Read Easier: Simplified Reading for Everyone

Not everyone who struggles with web reading has a specific diagnosed condition. Eye fatigue, attention difficulties, information overload, and simply uncomfortable page designs affect many people. [Read Easier](/apps/read-easier/) is a general-purpose reading accessibility extension that:

- **Simplifies page layouts** by removing distracting elements like sidebars, pop-ups, and excessive navigation.
- **Applies clean, readable typography** with optimized font sizes, line heights, and margins.
- **Offers reading guides** such as line highlighting and focus modes that help you track your position on the page.
- **Reduces visual clutter** so the content you are trying to read stands out clearly.

Read Easier works as an excellent complement to Safari's built-in Reader Mode, providing more customization options and working on pages where Reader Mode is not available.

## How to Choose the Right Accessibility Extension

**If you have dyslexia:** Start with the [Dyslexia](/apps/dyslexia/) extension. Its dyslexia-specific font and spacing adjustments target the exact challenges you face.

**If you have age-related vision changes:** [Presbyopia](/apps/presbyopia/) is designed specifically for you, with intelligent text enlargement that does not break page layouts.

**If you want a general reading improvement:** [Read Easier](/apps/read-easier/) provides broad readability enhancements that benefit anyone who finds most websites uncomfortable to read.

**If you browse at night or find bright screens uncomfortable:** Combine any of these with [Make It Dark Mode](/apps/make-it-dark-mode/) for reduced eye strain in low-light conditions. Read our full guide on [enabling dark mode on every website in Safari](/blog/safari-extensions/how-to-enable-dark-mode-on-every-website-in-safari/).

## Combining Accessibility Extensions

These extensions are not mutually exclusive. You can use Dyslexia for font adjustments alongside Make It Dark Mode for reduced brightness. Or combine Presbyopia's text enlargement with Read Easier's layout simplification for maximum comfort.

The key is to experiment with different combinations and find the setup that works best for your specific needs. Everyone's visual and cognitive preferences are different, and these extensions give you the flexibility to customize your experience accordingly.
