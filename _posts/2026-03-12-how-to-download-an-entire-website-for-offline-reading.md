## Why Download Websites for Offline Access?

Despite the widespread availability of internet access, there are many situations where you need web content but do not have connectivity. Flights without Wi-Fi, remote travel locations, underground commutes, areas with unreliable connections, or simply wanting to reduce your screen time while still having reference material available.

Downloading entire websites for offline reading solves this problem. Instead of saving individual pages one at a time, you can capture a complete website — with all its pages, images, and linked resources — for browsing locally without any internet connection.

## Two Extensions for Downloading Websites

Safari offers two dedicated extensions for downloading complete websites, each with different strengths.

### Website Downloader

[Website Downloader](/apps/website-downloader/) is designed for downloading entire websites or specific sections of a site. It crawls from a starting URL, follows internal links, and saves the pages and assets it finds to your local storage.

{% include blog-cta.html app_slug="website-downloader" %}

#### How to Use Website Downloader

1. **Install Website Downloader** from the App Store and enable it in Safari.
2. **Navigate to the website** you want to download. This is your starting URL.
3. **Click the Website Downloader icon** in the toolbar.
4. **Configure your download settings:**
   - **Depth level** — how many links deep the crawler should follow. A depth of 1 downloads the starting page and all pages linked from it. A depth of 2 follows links on those pages too, and so on.
   - **File types** — which types of files to include: HTML pages, images, CSS stylesheets, JavaScript, PDFs, and other resources.
   - **Domain restriction** — whether to stay within the same domain or also download linked resources from external domains.
   - **Download location** — where to save the files on your device.
5. **Start the download.** The extension will begin crawling and saving the site.
6. **Browse offline.** Open the downloaded files in Safari or any web browser to view the site without an internet connection.

### Site Slurpr

[Site Slurpr](/apps/site-slurpr/) takes a slightly different approach, focusing on making the offline browsing experience as seamless as possible. It packages downloaded sites in a format optimized for local viewing.

#### How Site Slurpr Differs

- **Optimized packaging** — downloaded sites are organized for easy navigation on your device.
- **Smart link handling** — internal links are rewritten to point to the local copies, so navigation works exactly as it would online.
- **Selective downloading** — choose specific sections or pages of a site rather than downloading everything.
- **Storage management** — easily see how much space each downloaded site occupies and delete old downloads.

## Best Use Cases for Offline Website Downloads

### Technical Documentation

Developer documentation (API references, framework guides, language documentation) is some of the most commonly accessed web content. Downloading documentation sites like MDN Web Docs, language references, or framework guides ensures you can code without interruption even when offline.

### Educational Content

Online courses, tutorials, and educational wikis can be downloaded for study sessions away from internet access. This is especially valuable for students in areas with limited connectivity.

### Travel Research

Before a trip, download travel guides, city maps, hotel information, and activity descriptions. Having this information available offline means you are not dependent on unreliable foreign Wi-Fi or expensive roaming data.

### Archival and Preservation

Websites change and disappear constantly. If a website contains content you want to preserve long-term — a blog you love, a recipe collection, historical information — downloading it creates a personal archive that survives even if the original goes offline. For checking if specific pages are already available through web archives, see our guide on [checking if a website is down and finding cached versions](/blog/safari-extensions/how-to-check-if-a-website-is-down-and-find-cached-versions/).

### Emergency Preparedness

Critical information like first aid guides, survival manuals, local emergency contacts, and offline maps should be available even when the internet is not.

## Tips for Effective Website Downloads

**Start small.** For your first download, try a small site or a specific section rather than an entire large website. This helps you understand the tool and gauge storage requirements.

**Set appropriate depth limits.** Setting the crawl depth too high on a large site can result in downloading thousands of pages you do not need. Start with a depth of 1 or 2 and increase if necessary.

**Check storage requirements.** A single web page might be just a few hundred kilobytes, but an entire documentation site with images can easily reach several gigabytes. Monitor your available storage before starting large downloads.

**Respect robots.txt and rate limits.** The crawler should respect the website's robots.txt file and avoid making requests too quickly. Most well-built download extensions handle this automatically, but be aware of the impact on the target server.

**Download during off-peak hours.** If you are on a metered connection, schedule large downloads for times when bandwidth is cheaper or unlimited.

**Keep downloads organized.** Create a folder structure for your downloaded sites — by topic, by date, or by project — so you can find specific content when you need it offline.
