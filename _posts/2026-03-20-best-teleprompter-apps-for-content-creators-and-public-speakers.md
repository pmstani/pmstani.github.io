A teleprompter used to be expensive studio equipment that only TV anchors and politicians could afford. Today, your iPhone or iPad can do the same job — often better — with the right app. Whether you are recording YouTube videos, delivering a keynote, or presenting to your team over video calls, a teleprompter app keeps your delivery smooth and your eyes on the camera.

This guide covers what to look for in a teleprompter app, the best options available in 2026, and practical tips for using one effectively.

## Why Use a Teleprompter App?

Reading from notes or a script taped next to your camera creates problems:

- **Your eyes drift.** Viewers can tell when you are reading from something off-screen. It breaks the connection.
- **You lose your place.** Glancing at notes means constantly finding where you left off.
- **Delivery feels choppy.** Start-stop reading makes you sound less confident and natural.
- **Multiple takes.** Without a prompter, you end up recording the same segment over and over to get it right.

A teleprompter app scrolls your script on the screen at a readable pace, so your eyes stay near the camera lens and your delivery stays consistent.

## What to Look for in a Teleprompter App

Not all teleprompter apps are created equal. Here are the features that matter:

### Adjustable Scroll Speed

Different scripts and speaking styles require different speeds. Look for an app that lets you set the scroll speed precisely and change it on the fly.

### Voice-Activated Scrolling

The best teleprompter apps listen to your voice and scroll at your natural speaking pace. If you pause, the text pauses. If you speed up, it keeps up. This is far more natural than fixed-speed scrolling.

### Mirror Mode

When using a physical teleprompter rig with a beam splitter, you need the text to display in mirror mode. Make sure the app supports this if you plan to use professional hardware.

### Font and Display Customization

You should be able to control font size, color, background, and margins. The text needs to be readable at a glance from your recording distance.

### Remote Control Support

Being able to start, stop, and adjust scrolling from a remote control or a second device is crucial for professional setups.

## Tips for Using a Teleprompter Effectively

Having a teleprompter app is only half the equation. Here is how to use it well:

### Write for Speaking, Not Reading

Teleprompter scripts should sound like natural speech. Use short sentences, contractions, and conversational language. Read your script out loud before recording and revise anything that sounds stiff.

### Practice Before You Record

Run through your script at least twice with the teleprompter before hitting record. This helps you get comfortable with the scroll speed and catch awkward phrases.

### Position the Device Near the Camera Lens

The closer the teleprompter text is to the camera lens, the more natural your eye line will appear. If the text is too far from the lens, it will look like you are reading from something off to the side.

### Use Larger Font Than You Think You Need

When recording, your device is usually a few feet away. Text that looks readable when you are holding the phone will be too small from recording distance. Increase the font size until you can read it comfortably from where you will be standing or sitting.

### Break Your Script into Sections

Instead of one long wall of text, break your script into sections with clear markers. This makes it easier to pause, re-record a section if needed, and maintain energy throughout longer recordings.

### Do Not Try to Read Every Word Perfectly

A teleprompter is a guide, not a cage. Feel free to ad-lib, rephrase, or skip a line if it flows better in the moment. The goal is natural delivery, not word-for-word recitation.
