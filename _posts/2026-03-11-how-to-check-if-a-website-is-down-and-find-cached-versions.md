## When Websites Go Dark

You click a link or type a familiar URL, and instead of the page you expected, you get an error. Connection timed out. 502 Bad Gateway. Page not found. The immediate question: is the website actually down, or is the problem on your end?

And if the site is genuinely down, is there a way to access the content you need anyway?

Two Safari extensions answer both of these questions instantly.

## Is It Down: Diagnose the Problem

[Is It Down](/apps/is-it-down/) answers the fundamental question: is this website down for everyone, or is it just you? The extension checks the website's status from external servers, so you get an objective answer rather than guessing based on your own connection.

{% include blog-cta.html app_slug="is-it-down" %}

### How to Use Is It Down

1. **Install Is It Down** from the App Store and enable it in Safari's extension settings.
2. **Visit the website** that appears to be having issues, or navigate to a page that is not loading.
3. **Click the Is It Down icon** in the Safari toolbar.
4. **View the results.** The extension will tell you whether the site is:
   - **Down for everyone** — the server is not responding to requests from any location.
   - **Down just for you** — the site is accessible from other locations, suggesting a local network, DNS, or ISP issue.
   - **Experiencing partial outage** — the site is intermittently available or slow.

### What to Do with the Results

**If the site is down for everyone:**
- There is nothing wrong on your end. The website's servers are experiencing issues.
- Check the site's social media accounts or status page for official updates.
- Use [Get Archive](/apps/get-archive/) to access a cached version of the content (see below).
- Set up [Auto-Refresh](/apps/auto-refresh/) to automatically check when the site comes back online. See our guide on [auto-scrolling and auto-refreshing pages in Safari](/blog/safari-extensions/how-to-auto-scroll-and-auto-refresh-pages-in-safari/).

**If it is down just for you:**
- Try clearing your Safari cache and cookies for that site.
- Flush your DNS cache (on Mac: `sudo dscacheutil -flushcache`).
- Try disabling your VPN if you are using one.
- Switch to a different network (cellular instead of Wi-Fi, or vice versa) to isolate the issue.
- Restart your router if other devices on your network also cannot reach the site.

## Get Archive: Access Cached and Archived Pages

When a web page is unavailable — whether temporarily down, permanently removed, or changed since you last visited — [Get Archive](/apps/get-archive/) helps you find saved versions from web archives and caches.

### How Get Archive Works

1. **Install Get Archive** from the App Store and enable it.
2. **Navigate to the URL** that is not loading or that has changed.
3. **Click the Get Archive icon** in the toolbar.
4. **Browse available versions.** The extension searches multiple archive sources, including:
   - **Wayback Machine** (Internet Archive) — the largest web archive, with billions of saved pages going back to the 1990s.
   - **Google Cache** — Google's most recently cached version of the page.
   - **Other archive services** that maintain copies of web content.
5. **Select a version** to view the archived copy of the page.

### When Get Archive Is Invaluable

**Research and citation.** If you cited a web page in a paper or article and the page has since been removed, Get Archive helps you find the version you originally referenced.

**Tracking changes.** Compare different archived versions of a page to see how content has changed over time. This is useful for monitoring competitors, tracking policy changes, or documenting the evolution of a website.

**Recovering lost content.** If your own website lost content due to a server failure, migration error, or accidental deletion, web archives may have a copy you can restore from.

**Accessing removed content.** News articles, blog posts, and social media pages sometimes get removed. Archives often retain copies.

**Historical research.** Studying how websites, companies, and organizations presented themselves at different points in history.

## Using Is It Down and Get Archive Together

These two extensions form a complete workflow for dealing with unavailable websites:

1. **Site not loading?** Click Is It Down to determine if the problem is global or local.
2. **Site is down for everyone?** Click Get Archive to find a cached or archived version of the content you need.
3. **Need to know when it comes back?** Use Auto-Refresh to periodically check the site.

This three-step approach ensures you can always access the information you need, even when the original source is temporarily or permanently unavailable.

## Tips for Working with Archives

- **Archives are snapshots.** They capture the page at a specific moment in time. Dynamic content, logged-in states, and interactive features will not be preserved.
- **Not everything is archived.** Pages behind paywalls, login walls, or robots.txt exclusions may not have archived copies.
- **Check multiple dates.** The Wayback Machine often has dozens or hundreds of snapshots of popular pages. Browse different dates to find the version closest to what you need.
- **Save important content proactively.** If you find a page with important information, save it yourself using [Save as PDF](/apps/save-as-pdf/) or [Website Downloader](/apps/website-downloader/) rather than relying on archives to have it later.
