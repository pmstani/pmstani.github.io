Small businesses often struggle with asset management. Tools go missing, warranties expire without notice, equipment maintenance gets deferred until something breaks, and come tax season, nobody can locate serial numbers or purchase receipts. These problems cost real money — in replacement purchases, downtime, and missed depreciation deductions.

The solution is a dedicated asset tracking system. For small businesses and independent operators, a well-designed mobile app is often more practical than enterprise inventory software. Here is how to approach equipment tracking effectively, and the tools that make it work.

## Why Small Businesses Need Asset Tracking

**Financial visibility.** Knowing exactly what you own and what it is worth helps you budget for replacements, plan capital expenditures, and maintain accurate books.

**Tax deductions.** Equipment depreciation is a legitimate business deduction, but you need accurate records — purchase dates, costs, and useful life — to claim it. Without tracking, you leave money on the table every year.

**Warranty management.** When a piece of equipment fails, the first question is always "Is it still under warranty?" With tracking, the answer is instant.

**Loss prevention.** Simply knowing what you have and where it is located reduces loss and theft. Employees are also more careful with tracked equipment.

**Insurance claims.** If disaster strikes, documented assets get reimbursed. Undocumented ones do not. This applies equally to business property and personal belongings — see our guide on [creating a home inventory for insurance](/blog/how-to-create-home-inventory-insurance/).

## How to Set Up Equipment Tracking with Equipt

[Equipt](/apps/equipt/) is a mobile-first asset tracking app designed for the scale and workflow of small businesses and independent professionals. Here is how to implement it:

### Step 1: Catalog Your Assets

Start with a complete audit of your equipment. Walk through your workspace and document every item worth tracking:

- **Name and description** — be specific enough to distinguish similar items
- **Make, model, and serial number** — critical for warranty claims and insurance
- **Purchase date and price** — needed for depreciation calculations
- **Location** — where the item is normally kept or assigned
- **Photo** — visual confirmation is invaluable during audits

### Step 2: Organize by Category

Group your assets logically based on your business type:

- **Computing equipment** — laptops, desktops, monitors, printers
- **Tools and machinery** — power tools, workshop equipment, specialized instruments
- **Vehicles** — company cars, vans, trailers
- **Furniture and fixtures** — desks, chairs, shelving, display units
- **Audio/visual** — cameras, projectors, microphones

### Step 3: Set Up Maintenance Schedules

For equipment that requires regular maintenance — vehicles, HVAC systems, compressors, printers — set reminders in Equipt. Preventive maintenance extends equipment life and prevents costly breakdowns.

### Step 4: Track Depreciation

Record the useful life and depreciation method for each asset. Common methods include:

- **Straight-line depreciation** — equal annual deduction over the useful life
- **Accelerated depreciation** — larger deductions in early years (MACRS for tax purposes)

Having this data organized in one place simplifies year-end tax preparation enormously.

### Step 5: Conduct Regular Audits

Schedule quarterly or semi-annual audits where you physically verify that tracked items are present and in their documented locations. Update records for items that have been moved, disposed of, or acquired since the last audit.

## Industries That Benefit Most

**Construction and trades.** Tools and equipment represent major capital investments. Tracking prevents job-site losses and ensures compliance with equipment certifications.

**Photography and videography.** Camera bodies, lenses, lighting, and accessories are high-value and frequently transported between locations.

**IT services and consulting.** Laptops, servers, networking equipment, and peripherals assigned to clients or employees need clear accountability.

**Rental businesses.** If you rent out equipment, tracking availability, condition, and maintenance history is essential.

**Food service.** Commercial kitchen equipment, refrigeration units, and food preparation tools all require maintenance tracking.

## Mobile vs. Desktop Asset Tracking

Enterprise asset management systems exist, but they are typically expensive, complex, and designed for organizations with dedicated IT staff. For businesses with fewer than 50 employees, a mobile-first approach offers several advantages:

- **Immediate documentation** — snap photos and enter data on the spot during audits
- **Always accessible** — check asset details from anywhere, not just the office
- **Lower cost** — no servers, no licenses, no implementation consulting fees
- **Faster adoption** — intuitive mobile interfaces require minimal training
