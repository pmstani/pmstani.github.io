There are plenty of reasons you might need to convert a photo to PDF on your iPhone. Submitting a receipt for reimbursement, sending a signed document, creating a photo portfolio, digitizing handwritten notes — all of these call for a clean PDF rather than a raw image file.

The good news is that you can do this without installing anything. The even better news is that with the right app, you can do it faster and with more control. This guide covers both approaches.

## Why Convert Photos to PDF?

PDFs have advantages over image files in many situations:

- **Universal compatibility.** PDFs look the same on every device and operating system.
- **Multi-page support.** You can combine multiple photos into a single document.
- **Smaller file size.** A PDF of a photo is often smaller than the original image, especially with compression.
- **Professional appearance.** PDFs look more polished than sending raw photos, especially for business documents.
- **Print consistency.** PDFs maintain exact dimensions and layout when printed.

## Method 1: Convert Photos to PDF Using the Built-In Print Dialog

iOS has a hidden PDF creation feature built into the print dialog. Here is how to use it:

### Single Photo to PDF

1. Open the **Photos** app and select the image you want to convert.
2. Tap the **Share** button (the square with an arrow).
3. Scroll down and tap **Print**.
4. On the print preview screen, use a two-finger pinch-out gesture on the preview image. This opens the image as a PDF.
5. Tap the **Share** button again to save, email, or send the PDF.

### Multiple Photos to PDF

1. In the Photos app, tap **Select** and choose multiple images.
2. Tap the **Share** button.
3. Tap **Print**.
4. Pinch-out on the preview to create a multi-page PDF.
5. Share or save the resulting PDF.

This method works, but it has limitations:

- No control over page size, margins, or orientation.
- No ability to reorder pages.
- No compression options.
- The pinch-to-zoom trick is not intuitive — most people do not know it exists.

## Method 2: Use the Files App

The Files app offers another built-in approach:

1. Save your photos to the **Files** app (share from Photos and choose "Save to Files").
2. Open the Files app and navigate to the folder containing your images.
3. Tap the **three-dot menu** and select **Select**.
4. Choose the images you want to include.
5. Tap the **three-dot menu** again and select **Create PDF**.

The Files app creates a PDF with each image on a separate page. It is straightforward but still lacks options for page sizing, ordering, and quality settings.

## Method 3: Use Shortcuts

Apple's Shortcuts app can automate photo-to-PDF conversion:

1. Open the **Shortcuts** app.
2. Create a new shortcut.
3. Add the **Select Photos** action (enable "Select Multiple").
4. Add the **Make PDF** action.
5. Add a **Share** action or **Save File** action.
6. Run the shortcut whenever you need to convert photos.

This is more flexible than the other built-in methods because you can customize the workflow, but it requires some setup and familiarity with Shortcuts.

## Common Use Cases

### Scanning Receipts and Invoices

Take photos of paper receipts and convert them to PDF for expense reports. The PDF is easier to email, upload to expense systems, and archive than individual photos.

### Digitizing Handwritten Notes

Photograph your notebook pages and create a PDF that you can annotate, share, or store in your note-taking app.

### Creating Photo Portfolios

Photographers and designers can assemble a selection of work into a multi-page PDF portfolio that looks professional on any device.

### Submitting Signed Documents

When you need to sign a printed document, take a photo and convert it to PDF before sending it back. The PDF looks cleaner and more official than a raw photo.

### Archiving Whiteboards

After a brainstorming session, photograph the whiteboard and convert the images to a PDF document that can be shared with the team and stored for reference.

## Tips for Better Photo-to-PDF Results

1. **Use good lighting.** Even lighting without shadows produces cleaner PDFs.
2. **Shoot straight on.** Photograph documents from directly above to avoid perspective distortion.
3. **Use the highest resolution.** More pixels mean crisper text in the final PDF.
4. **Crop before converting.** Remove unnecessary background from the photo before adding it to the PDF.
5. **Organize before converting.** Get your photos in the right order in your Camera Roll before selecting them.
