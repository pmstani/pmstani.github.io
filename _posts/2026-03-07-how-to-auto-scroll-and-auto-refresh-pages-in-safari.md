## Automate Repetitive Browsing Tasks in Safari

Two of the most common repetitive actions in web browsing are scrolling through long pages and manually refreshing pages to check for updates. Both tasks are simple individually, but when you do them hundreds of times a day, they add up to a significant drain on your time and attention.

Safari extensions can automate both of these tasks, letting you browse hands-free and monitor pages without constantly hitting the refresh button. Here is how to set up automatic scrolling and auto-refreshing in Safari.

## Auto-Scroll: Hands-Free Reading

[Auto-Scroll](/apps/auto-scroll/) is a Safari extension that scrolls web pages automatically at a speed you control. Instead of manually scrolling or repeatedly pressing the spacebar, you activate the extension and let the page flow smoothly at your reading pace.

{% include blog-cta.html app_slug="auto-scroll" %}

### How to Use Auto-Scroll

1. **Install Auto-Scroll** from the App Store and enable it in Safari's extension settings.
2. **Navigate to a long page** — an article, a feed, documentation, or any content that requires sustained scrolling.
3. **Click the Auto-Scroll icon** in the Safari toolbar to start automatic scrolling.
4. **Adjust the speed** using the extension's controls. Start slow and increase the pace until it matches your natural reading speed.
5. **Pause or stop** scrolling at any time by clicking the icon again or using the keyboard shortcut.

### Best Use Cases for Auto-Scroll

**Reading long articles and documentation.** Technical documentation, longform journalism, and ebook-style content are much more comfortable to read when the page scrolls automatically at a consistent pace. No more interrupted reading flow from manual scrolling.

**Browsing social media feeds.** Scrolling through long feeds on social media or news aggregators becomes effortless. Set a comfortable speed and skim through content without lifting a finger.

**Presenting web content.** If you are showing a web page during a presentation or screen share, auto-scroll provides a smooth, professional scrolling experience instead of jerky manual scrolling.

**Hands-free reading while eating or exercising.** Sometimes you want to read while your hands are occupied. Auto-scroll makes this possible.

### Tips for Auto-Scroll

- Start with a **slower speed** and gradually increase it. Most people overestimate their reading speed initially.
- Use **keyboard shortcuts** if available to quickly pause and resume without reaching for the mouse.
- On pages with expandable content (like "Show More" buttons), pause auto-scroll, expand the content, then resume.

## Auto-Refresh: Keep Pages Up to Date

[Auto-Refresh](/apps/auto-refresh/) is a Safari extension that reloads web pages on a schedule you define. Set a refresh interval — every 5 seconds, every 30 seconds, every 5 minutes, or any interval you choose — and the extension handles the rest.

### How to Use Auto-Refresh

1. **Install Auto-Refresh** from the App Store and enable it in Safari's extension settings.
2. **Navigate to the page** you want to keep refreshed.
3. **Click the Auto-Refresh icon** in the toolbar.
4. **Set your refresh interval.** Choose a time that makes sense for your use case — frequent refreshes for rapidly changing content, less frequent for slower-updating pages.
5. **Let it run.** The extension will reload the page at your specified interval until you deactivate it.

### Best Use Cases for Auto-Refresh

**Monitoring dashboards and analytics.** If you keep a web-based dashboard open — analytics, server status, social media metrics — auto-refresh ensures you always see the latest data without manually reloading.

**Tracking auction and sale pages.** When monitoring eBay auctions, flash sales, or limited-stock product pages, auto-refresh keeps you informed of changes in real time.

**Following live events.** Live blogs, score updates, news feeds during breaking events, and other live content benefit from automatic refreshing so you never miss an update.

**Keeping session alive.** Some web applications time out after a period of inactivity. Auto-refresh prevents session timeouts by periodically reloading the page, keeping you logged in.

**Monitoring website availability.** Pair Auto-Refresh with the [Is It Down](/apps/is-it-down/) extension to continuously check whether a site has come back online after an outage. Learn more in our guide on [checking if a website is down](/blog/safari-extensions/how-to-check-if-a-website-is-down-and-find-cached-versions/).

### Tips for Auto-Refresh

- **Set reasonable intervals.** Refreshing every second is rarely necessary and can trigger rate limiting on some sites. Match the interval to how frequently the content actually changes.
- **Be mindful of bandwidth.** Frequent refreshes on media-heavy pages consume bandwidth. Consider using shorter intervals only for lightweight pages.
- **Combine tabs strategically.** You can run auto-refresh on multiple tabs simultaneously, each with different intervals suited to the content.

## Using Auto-Scroll and Auto-Refresh Together

These two extensions complement each other well. For example:

- Auto-refresh a news feed page every few minutes to load new content, then auto-scroll through the updated feed to browse new items hands-free.
- Keep a live event page refreshing automatically while auto-scrolling through accumulated updates.
